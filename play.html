Opportunity Attack

If a player or encounter moves away from a player or encounter, it triggers an Opportunity Attack.

In 5e, this is a Reaction used to make one melee attack against a visible enemy that willingly moves out of your reach (typically 5 ft).

  • The attack occurs right before the target leaves your range.
  • You cannot use this if the enemy uses the Disengage action.
  • You cannot use this if the enemy is moved involuntarily (e.g., pushed, dropped, or teleported).

Ready an Action

  • Reaction Cost: Holding an action uses your Reaction for this round.
  • Concentration: If you Ready a script/spell, you must maintain concentration on it until released.
  • Expiration: If the trigger doesn't occur before your next turn, the action is lost.

Animal Handling Rules

1. Claiming / Befriending an Animal

When you first try to make an animal your companion, roll Animal Handling (1d20 + Animal Handling modifier)

  • Success | Animal accepts you as a handler
  • Fail by 1–4 | Animal distrusts you (try again later)
  • Fail by 5+ | Animal flees or becomes hostile and is no longer a personal tie pet

2. Bonding Period

Even if claimed, the bond must grow.

  • Spend 3–7 in-game days caring for the animal.
  • Make 1 Animal Handling check per day.
  • 3 successes = bonded companion.
  • Failure does not break the bond attempt but slows progress.

3. Training the Pet

Each command requires training. Roll Animal Handling once per training session (usually 1 day). 2 successes = command learned.

When giving commands in stressful situations, roll Animal Handling.
