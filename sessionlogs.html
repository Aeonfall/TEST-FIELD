Calendar of Aeonfall

How Time Endures After the World Broke.

Time did not stop when the world fractured. It adapted. The Aeonfall Calendar is a reconstructed system derived from pre-collapse records, Custodian archives, and long-standing cultural consensus.

Time Structure

  • Year: 360 days
  • Months: 12 months, 30 days each
  • Week: 7 days
  • Moons: 2 (Selura, Veythar)
  • Seasons: 4 seasonal markers

The Four Eras

  • The Age of Concord: The height of Aeon. An era of unified knowledge.
  • The Sundering: The Calamity of Aeon. Widespread system failure. Dates from this era are unreliable.
  • The Interregnum: Early Aeonfall. An age of survival and fragmentation.
  • The Shattered Dawn (Current Era): Civilization returns in altered form. Most modern dating uses this era (SD) as its reference.

The Two Moons

  • Selura (The Silver Moon):     Cycle: 30 days. Associations: Memory, history, reflection. Selura’s full cycle aligns with the monthly calendar. Many record-keepers prefer to finalize archives during its full phase.
  • Veythar (The Red Moon):     Cycle: 15 days. Associations: Change, danger, the Eternal Heart. Veythar completes two cycles per month. Its rise is often linked to instability and phenomena.

Seasonal Markers & Observances

  • Dawnsreach: Balance of light and dark. Days begin to grow longer.
  • Solflare: Longest day. Peak heat, labor, and travel.
  • Duskfall: Balance returns. Nights begin to lengthen.
  • Nightvein: Longest night. A time of endurance and reflection.
  • Prime Day: Trade, family gathering. (1st of Luminar)
  • Forge Day: Industry, craftsmanship. (10th of Metallon)
  • Ashlight Commemoration: Remembrance of the Sundering. (30th of Ashveil)
  • Veilwake: Reflection on loss and survival. (30th of Heartveil)
